Life & Legacy

1928

Early Life

Born in Philadelphia to Jewish parents. His father William Chomsky was a noted Hebrew scholar who influenced his early linguistic interests.

1945-1955

Academic Formation

Studied at the University of Pennsylvania, where he developed his early theories in linguistics under Zellig Harris. Completed his Ph.D. dissertation on transformational analysis.

1955-1965

Revolutionary Theories

Joined MIT and published "Syntactic Structures," revolutionizing the field of linguistics with his theory of transformational generative grammar.

1965-1975

Political Activism

Emerged as a prominent critic of U.S. foreign policy, particularly regarding the Vietnam War. Published influential works combining linguistics and political analysis.

1975-2000

Expanding Influence

Continued developing linguistic theories while becoming one of the world's leading public intellectuals and critics of U.S. foreign policy and media.

2000-Present

Contemporary Impact

Remains active in political discourse and academic work, publishing on linguistics, politics, and media criticism while continuing to influence public debate.
